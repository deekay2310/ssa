Stainless Steel is an austenitic chromium-nickel-manganese stainless steel which was developed to conserve nickel.Stainless Steel is a lower cost alternative to conventional Cr-Ni stainless steels such as 301 and 304. Nickel is replaced by additions of manganese and nitrogen. It is non-hardenable by thermal treatment, but may be cold worked to high tensile strengths. Stainless Steel is essentially nonmagnetic in the annealed condition and becomes magnetic when cold worked.
